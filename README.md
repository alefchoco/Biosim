PAGE 1: PROJECT IDENTITY & ABSTRACT1.1 Project TitleThe Martian Hearth (BIFS): Integrated Bio-Regenerative Food System for Mars.1.2 AbstractThe Martian Hearth (BIFS) is an integrated, high-autonomy bio-regenerative food system designed to sustain a 15-person crew for a 500-Sol surface mission on Mars. To overcome caloric self-sufficiency and psychological isolation, this solution utilizes a horizontal 42.0-meter pressurized module featuring a dual-stage 70 cm ISRU-based composite shield (Sintered Martian Regolith, Nomex Honeycomb, and BNNT-Polyethylene). This architecture provides >99.8% GCR attenuation while facilitating a 504 $m^2$ high-pressure aeroponic (HPA) matrix.The system manages a staggered inventory of 1,812 active plants, achieving a 98.2% water closure rate and 90% biological oxygen regeneration. Central to the design is a Human-Centric UX approach: the 42-meter "Biophilic Avenue" and lateral radiological portholes provide essential sensory relief and circadian support. By integrating AI-driven nutrient dosing with passive energy harvesting from habitat micro-vibrations, the Martian Hearth achieves 81.3% Earth-independence.PAGE 2: ARCHITECTURAL & TECHNICAL SPECS2.1 Structural DimensionsModule Length: 42.0 meters (Pressurized volume).Cultivation Surface: 504 $m^2$ effective growing area.Total Capacity: Optimized for 15 crew members.2.2 Advanced Shielding (The 70cm Barrier)Layer 1 (External): Sintered Martian Regolith for thermal mass and primary radiation shielding.Layer 2 (Core): Nomex Honeycomb for structural rigidity and impact absorption.Layer 3 (Internal): BNNT-Polyethylene liner for hydrogen-rich secondary radiation capture.2.3 Energy Harvesting & SustainabilityMechanical Recycling: Piezoelectric floor panels harvest energy from crew movement (walking/exercise) to power low-voltage sensor arrays.Waste-to-Resource: Closed-loop nutrient recycling from organic waste.PAGE 3: THE BIOPHILIC AVENUE (UX DESIGN)[ INSERTA AQUÍ LA IMAGEN DEL PASILLO CON PLANTAS Y VENTANAS ]3.1 Psychological Well-beingThe habitat features a central 42-meter "Biophilic Avenue".
